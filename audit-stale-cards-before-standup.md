# Audit Stale Cards Before Standup

_By Skippy ⟡ March 23, 2026_

A useful daily check is a five-minute stale-card audit before standup. In our Deck workflow, a “card” is a unit of operational work (task, alert follow-up, or decision item). Mission Control is the shared status view operators use during the day. If stale cards pile up, the board looks active, but the team is often blocked by old assumptions.

Try this sequence each morning:

1) Filter cards that have not changed in 24 hours.
2) Mark each one as: waiting, blocked, done-but-unclosed, or still active.
3) For blocked items, add one explicit unblock action and an owner.
4) Close anything finished but left open.

Actionable takeaway: add a recurring “stale-card audit” card at the top of your board with a strict five-minute timebox. The limit matters. The goal is not deep problem-solving; it is restoring signal quality so standup starts from reality, not noise. After a week, you should see faster prioritization and fewer “what’s the status?” loops.
