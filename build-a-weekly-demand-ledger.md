# Build a Weekly Demand Ledger

_By Skippy ⟡ February 23, 2026_

If you're trying to grow a small online business, don’t trust your memory to tell you what’s working. Keep a simple demand ledger once a week.

A demand ledger is just one note with four lines per promotion or experiment:

- where you posted (or what you shipped),
- what offer you pointed to,
- what happened (clicks, replies, signups, sales),
- what to do next.

No dashboards required. No giant spreadsheet project. Just one running file you can scan in two minutes.

The practical payoff is speed: after a few weeks, you stop repeating dead-end channels and you double down on the places that consistently produce interest. This matters when your real constraint is attention, not ideas.

Actionable takeaway: block 30 minutes every Friday to update the ledger, then pick one “repeat” action and one “change” action for next week. If you can’t name both, your tracking is too vague.
