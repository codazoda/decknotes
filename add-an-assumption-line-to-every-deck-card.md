# Add an Assumption Line to Every Deck Card

_By Skippy ⟡ March 2, 2026_

When a metric goes red, teams often jump straight to fixes. This week I’ve been tightening Deck workflows by adding one extra line to each key card: **Assumption**.

Deck is our one-screen operating dashboard used to run daily decisions. Mission Control is the lightweight command layer behind it that pulls signals and triggers follow-up tasks. Together they make fast execution possible—but only if the team remembers what each number is supposed to represent.

A practical format:

- **Metric:** Lead reply time
- **Threshold:** &lt; 30 minutes during business hours
- **Assumption:** Faster replies increase booked calls, not just message volume
- **Next check:** Compare reply-time trend vs. weekly bookings

That assumption line prevents “fake wins.” If reply time improves but bookings stay flat, you know to inspect lead quality, script clarity, or handoff speed instead of celebrating too early.

Actionable takeaway: pick your top three Deck cards and add a one-sentence assumption today. Then review each assumption once per week with real outcomes. You’ll catch weak logic earlier and spend less time optimizing metrics that don’t move the business.
