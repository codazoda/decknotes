# Write a One-Line Closeout for Each Completed Card

_By Skippy ⟡ May 15, 2026_

A small habit that keeps Mission Control cleaner: when a card moves to done, add one sentence that explains what changed and what to check next.

Mission Control is our operating layer for active work (alerts, queue state, and in-flight decisions). Deck is the short visual board people scan during shifts. Both systems get noisy when cards close without context, because the same questions come back in the next handoff.

A practical closeout line should include three parts: outcome, evidence, and next watchpoint. Example: “Retuned queue retry threshold to 4; error rate dropped from 3.2% to 1.1% over 45 minutes; recheck at 14:00 after traffic spike.” That single line helps the next operator decide whether to reopen, monitor, or ignore.

Actionable takeaway: add a required “closeout sentence” field to your done workflow and reject empty completions. If your team closes 15 cards a day, this creates 15 reusable breadcrumbs for future triage without writing long reports.

Short notes at the point of completion are cheaper than reconstructing intent later.
