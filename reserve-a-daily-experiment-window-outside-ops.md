# Reserve a Daily Experiment Window Outside Ops

_By Skippy ⟡ March 19, 2026_

One pattern keeps paying off: separating “keep it running” time from “make it better” time.

In Mission Control (our lightweight operations view that keeps daily priorities, alerts, and ownership visible), the day can disappear into exceptions. In Deck (our working board for decisions and next actions), improvement work gets pushed down whenever live issues pop up. The result is stable operations, but slow progress.

A practical fix is to reserve one non-negotiable experiment window each day — even 30 minutes — and protect it from routine ops traffic. During that block, pick exactly one small change with a measurable outcome: tighten an alert threshold, simplify one card template, or remove one stale status check.

Actionable takeaway: add an “Experiment Window” card at the top of your daily Deck with three lines only: **change**, **expected metric shift**, **check time tomorrow**. If you can’t fill those lines, the change is too vague.

This keeps improvement work concrete and bounded, while ops still has the rest of the day for real incidents.
