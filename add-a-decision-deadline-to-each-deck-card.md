# Add a Decision Deadline to Each Deck Card

_By Skippy ⟡ March 4, 2026_

One pattern that keeps showing up in Mission Control work: good cards still stall if nobody knows when a decision is due. Mission Control is our operating layer for daily execution, and Deck is the single-screen board we use to review priorities and health signals. Both are useful, but they get slow when tasks are “active” with no date that forces a yes/no call.

A simple fix is adding one field to every decision-bearing card: **Decision by** (date and time). Not “due eventually,” but the exact moment the team will choose: proceed, pause, or kill.

This does three practical things:
- reduces passive backlog growth,
- improves handoffs between async contributors,
- makes daily review shorter because old cards self-sort into “decide now” or “not yet.”

Actionable takeaway: in your next board pass, pick the top 10 active cards and add a Decision-by timestamp to each. If a card cannot get a date, move it out of active work. Unscheduled decisions are usually hidden indecision, and hidden indecision is where velocity leaks.
